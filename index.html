I am a postdoc at Stanford with Stephen Boyd and Arun Majumdar.

My research focuses on the design and development of novel algorithms for large-scale optimization, with a focus on applications in electrical energy systems. Prior to Stanford, I received a PhD in Machine Learning from CMU and worked at Google building new products and systems in search, ads and several new areas.

My most recent project (and a major part of my thesis) is Epsilon, a general convex programming framework built on fast proximal and linear operators. The high-level goal is to develop a new class of optimization tools for rapid development of machine learning models and other applications.
